Chow Mein Noodles

Description

Egg Hakka Noodles is a popular Indo-Chinese dish loved across the country. Packed with a variety of vegetables, it’s a healthy option that provides plenty of nutrients. It’s also a quick and easy dish to prepare, perfect when you're short on time. One of its biggest perks is that kids will happily eat the veggies without any complaints

  • 200g Hakka noodles
  • 1 Onion
  • chopped (for garnish)
  • 2-3 spring onions
  • for cooking
  • Salt
  • 1 tbsp red chili sauce (optional)
  • 1 tbsp vinegar
  • 1 tbsp green chili sauce
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • shredded ½ capsicum (bell pepper)
  • carrot

Steps to cook

  1. Boil water, add salt and oil, then cook 200g Hakka noodles for 5-6 minutes. Drain, rinse with cold water, drizzle oil, and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a wok, scramble 2 eggs with a pinch of salt, and set aside.
  3. Add 1 sliced onion, stir-fry until soft, then add julienned carrots, shredded cabbage, and sliced capsicum. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
  4. In the same wok, heat oil and sauté 2-3 chopped garlic cloves and 1-inch chopped ginger for 30 seconds.
  5. Lower heat, add 2 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp green chili sauce, 1 tbsp vinegar, and optional red chili sauce. Mix well.
  6. toss gently for 1-2 minutes. Garnish with spring onions and serve.
